The Doctoral Program in **Information Technologies, Communication Networks and Cybersecurity** addresses one of the most **dynamic and strategically critical** domains in contemporary science and technology. As **digital infrastructure** becomes the backbone of modern society, the demand for advanced research in **network architectures**, **data transmission technologies**, and **cybersecurity frameworks** has never been more urgent.

This program prepares doctoral candidates to conduct **pioneering research** at the intersection of information technology, telecommunications, and information security. Core research themes encompass **next-generation network design**, **wireless and optical communication systems**, **protocol engineering**, **cloud and edge computing infrastructures**, **cryptographic methods**, **intrusion detection systems**, and the development of **resilient cybersecurity policies and technologies**.

Doctoral candidates are expected to produce an original dissertation that makes a **substantive and demonstrable contribution** to the knowledge base of the field. The program fosters **interdisciplinary thinking**, encouraging researchers to integrate perspectives from computer science, electrical engineering, mathematics, and policy studies to address **complex, real-world security and infrastructure challenges**.

Graduates are well-positioned to pursue **academic appointments**, lead research teams within **national and European research programs**, and assume **senior advisory roles** in governmental agencies and private sector organizations responsible for critical information infrastructure.
