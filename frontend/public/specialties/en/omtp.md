The Doctoral Program in **Organization and Management in Telecommunications and Posts** is an advanced interdisciplinary program at the nexus of **management science**, **organizational theory**, and the rapidly evolving **telecommunications and postal sectors**. The program addresses the growing complexity of managing **technologically intensive service organizations** in competitive, regulated, and digitally transforming environments.

Doctoral research within this program encompasses **strategic management of telecommunications enterprises**, **regulatory economics**, **organizational behavior in networked industries**, **digital transformation governance**, **public policy** for telecommunications and postal services, **innovation management**, and the application of contemporary managerial frameworks to sector-specific challenges. Particular attention is given to the evolving role of **digital platforms**, **e-commerce logistics**, and the **convergence of communication and postal services** in shaping new organizational models.

Candidates are expected to produce doctoral dissertations that advance **theoretical understanding** and provide **evidence-based insights** applicable to managerial decision-making, regulatory policy, and organizational development within the sector. The program draws on established traditions in management research while engaging critically with **emerging paradigms of the digital economy**.
